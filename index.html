Environment Monitoring

Information regarding air quality has great importance for protecting human health and controlling pollution. The number of static monitoring stations used is very less compared to the coverage required. Urban air quality changes non-linearly and depends on many factors, such as meteorological, land uses, traffic, and human mobility. Due to the insufficient amount of data it is difficult to model these additional factors. Air quality also changes with time and location. As a result, inferring real-time air quality of an arbitrary location using sparse monitoring data becomes critical. Two monitoring station, merely in 2km apart can show a certain inflection in their reading. Due to this fact forecasting of air quality is a nontrivial problem. Considering above observations there is a need for an ICT based solution using low cost air quality monitoring devices which can be used for inference as well as for forecasting air quality.

Activity recognition

In recent times sensor technologies are being widely used to create an interactive smart environment. Significant number of sensors deployed in a wireless sensor network will collect information regarding the change of state in their surroundings. This information in case of individuals can be interpreted as a change in activity. In recent years advancement in sensor technology has contributed significantly in designing sensor based applications. These low-power, low-cost, high-capacities, miniature sensors can be used to solve real life problems by identifying human activities. For example, in case of security and surveillance, human activity recognition techniques can be used to identify threats from terrorist attacks. As the average age is increasing in developed and developing countries, activity recognition can be used for protecting aged people from accidental injuries. For patients, in hospital or in-house, these can be used for remote monitoring. Designing energy efficient home or office will have significant use of human activity recognition techniques.

Health Sensing

Wireless technology has completely transformed the way we live, but health care is yet to enter the digital age at least at remote areas. By harnessing innovation in the wireless space along with cloud computing and pervasive technologies such as ubiquitous sensing and data analytics we can fundamentally shift the paradigm in health care delivery and dramatically improve the health care services at rural areas. Ultimately, we have the opportunity to create a new “infrastructure-independence” model of health care, which translates into the right care, at the right time, wherever people need it. Also putting cloud computing with it makes the health data to be monitored at any comfortable places or devices. Wireless health encompasses end-to-end solutions that facilitate continuous access to health care information, expert advice, or therapeutic intervention enabled by remote sensing, ubiquitous telecommunications networks, and smart systems and platforms

Visible Light Communiction (LiFi)

Visible light communication (VLC) is becoming an alternative choice for next-generation wireless technology by offering low cost, unregulated bandwidth and ubiquitous infrastructures support. This technology is envisioned to be used in a wide range of applications both indoor as well as outdoor. Visible Light Communication (VLC) uses light emitting diodes (LEDs), for the dual role of illumination and data transmission. With this leading edge technology, data including video and audio, internet traffic etc. can be transmitted at high speeds using LED light. Using LEDs is helping to drive this technology in the form of Visible Light Communication (VLC).
